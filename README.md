# Academic Poster — SDI Implementation for Emergency Mapping: Dominican Republic

Seminar poster summarizing and presenting the research of Rosario Michel et al. (2023),
developed as part of coursework at the Centre for Geographic Information Science (CGIS),
University of Punjab, Lahore.

---

## About This Work

This repository contains an academic poster prepared for a departmental seminar at
CGIS, University of Punjab. The poster summarizes and visually communicates the
findings of the following peer-reviewed publication:

> Rosario Michel, G.; Gonzalez-Campos, M.E.; Manzano Aybar, F.; Crompvoets, J.
> *Assessing SDI Implementation Scenarios to Facilitate Emergency Mapping Operations
> in the Dominican Republic.*
> ISPRS Int. J. Geo-Inf. 2023, 12, 184.
> https://doi.org/10.3390/ijgi12050184

The goal of this exercise was to develop skills in scientific communication — extracting
core methodology, results, and implications from a primary research paper and presenting
them in a structured visual format for an academic audience.

## Original Paper — Summary

**Research Question:**
How should an SDI be implemented to effectively facilitate Emergency Mapping
Operations (EMO) in the Dominican Republic — a Small Island Developing State (SIDS)
highly exposed to hurricanes, earthquakes, and floods?

**Methodology:**
The study applies Multi-Actor Multi-Criteria Analysis (MAMCA) — a structured
framework that captures and compares objectives across multiple stakeholder groups
using the Analytical Hierarchy Process (AHP) for scenario ranking.

**Stakeholder Groups (25 participants total):**

| Group | n |
|---|---|
| Government sector | 5 |
| Emergency Mapping Team (EIGEO/SINI) | 7 |
| Private sector | 8 |
| Academia | 5 |

**Three SDI Implementation Scenarios Evaluated:**

| Scenario | Description |
|---|---|
| Going Concern | Baseline — current state of SDI development, no formal policy |
| Increasing Hierarchy | Government-led, top-down legal and regulatory framework |
| Increasing Network | Networked governance, higher stakeholder participation, reduced government funding monopoly |

**Key Criteria Across Stakeholders:**
Up-to-date data, public-private partnerships, effective communication,
data standardization, institutional framework, funding availability.

---

## Results

**Overall preferred scenario: Increasing Network**

| Stakeholder | Most Preferred Scenario | Score |
|---|---|---|
| Government | Increasing Network | 46% |
| Emergency Mapping Team | Increasing Hierarchy | 38% |
| Private Sector | Increasing Network | 48% |
| Academia | Increasing Network | 44% |

**Core finding:** A well-coordinated inter-organizational network through SDI —
empowering all stakeholders including private sector, academia, and EMTs — is the
most suitable path for SDI implementation to support emergency mapping in the DR.

A phased implementation roadmap was proposed: begin with Increasing Hierarchy
to establish legal and institutional foundations, then transition toward Increasing
Network for long-term collaborative governance.

## Research Framework (Poster)

The poster visualizes the end-to-end research framework:

```
Context/Problem → Objectives → Methodology → Criteria
       ↓
   MAMCA + Case Study → Three Scenarios
       ↓
   AHP Pairwise Comparison across Stakeholder Groups
       ↓
   Scenario Rankings per Stakeholder → Multi-actor View
       ↓
   Implementation Implications and Roadmap
```

## What I Learned From This Exercise

Preparing this poster required close reading of a methodologically complex paper
involving multi-criteria decision analysis, stakeholder theory, and SDI governance
frameworks — topics directly relevant to applied GIS research and spatial data
policy. The exercise built skills in:

- Distilling multi-stakeholder quantitative results into clear visual communication
- Understanding MAMCA and AHP as decision-support methodologies applicable
  to SDI, land administration, and disaster risk management contexts
- Communicating spatial policy research to a non-specialist academic audience
